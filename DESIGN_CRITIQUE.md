# Wingman — the dossier

## What it is

Not a travel app. **A private travel office**, working a trip through its whole arc:

> **Plan** it · **Prepare** it · **Stay with you** through it · **Catch it** when it falls

The fourth is the one nobody else does. When a flight slips, everything downstream slips with it — the connection, the hotel check-in, the dinner, the car that's now an hour early. **Handling that cascade is the product.** Everything else earns the right to be trusted with it.

---

## What luxury actually means here

Luxury is **not minimalism**. My last proposal stripped the app toward a notification screen; that was wrong. An Aman doesn't hand you an empty lobby.

Luxury is:

- **Considered, not sparse.** Density is fine — every element has been *chosen*.
- **Prepared in advance.** The dossier is already on the table when you arrive.
- **Discreet.** It doesn't announce its own competence.
- **Never gamified.** This is the big one.

### The things that break it right now

**Wingman Points.** A tier ladder — EXPLORER → FLYER → NAVIGATOR → ELITE — awarding **200 points for signing up** and **100 for enabling push notifications.** A private office does not give you a badge for letting it contact you. **Delete it entirely.** Not "tone it down." Delete it.

**"Share your ROI ↗"** — asking a client to broadcast how much money their concierge saved them. No serious service has ever asked this. Delete.

**The stats bar** — "16 TRIPS · 6,400 MILES · 81 NIGHTS AWAY". A fitness tracker. Your travel office does not congratulate you on your step count.

**Suggestion chips** — "Any disruption risks?" A product that claims to already know, asking you to prompt it about the thing it claims to already know.

**"Compound the moat"**, "46 trips protected", "100% rescue accept rate" *(all fixed)* — self-flattery. The discretion rule: **a great concierge never tells you they're doing a great job.** You notice.

## The structure: the trip is the product

Not four tabs of features. **A collection of dossiers**, plus the standing brief.

### 1. The Brief — *what is happening right now*

Today, and only today. One editorial screen. Not a dashboard.

- **The state of things**, in a sentence. *"All quiet."* / *"AA4436 is delayed 40 minutes. Your connection still holds — I'm watching it."*
- **What needs you** — decisions, and nothing else. Usually empty. That emptiness is the promise being kept.
- **What's next** — the coming trip, with what's already been handled.
- **The conversation** — always there, below.

### 2. The Dossier — *one per trip, and it changes as the trip does*

**This is where the product lives.** The same document reads differently depending on where you are in the arc:

| Phase | The dossier shows |
|---|---|
| **Planning** | What you've told Wingman. What it's found. What it's holding. |
| **Prepared** (weeks out) | The itinerary as a printed folder. Entry requirements — from the authority, never asserted. Weather outlook. Standing orders: *what I may do without asking.* |
| **In motion** (day-of) | Gate, countdown, ground transport, the lock screen. The rest recedes. |
| **After** | The debrief. Expenses. What Wingman learned about you. |

One document. Four chapters. Which one you see is decided by the calendar, not by you tapping.

### 3. The Situation — *the cascade*

**The crown jewel, and it deserves a room of its own.**

When a leg breaks, this is not a notification. It is a **full-bleed takeover**: the chain of consequences, laid out, with what Wingman has already done and the one decision it needs.

> **BA1476 · cancelled**
>
> *Downstream, at risk:*
> — Connection to Edinburgh (18:40) — **missed**
> — Hoxton Edinburgh, arriving after 23:00 — *I've told them; the room is held*
> — Dinner at Timberyard, 20:30 — *cancelled on your behalf, no fee*
> — Car, 17:50 — *pushed to 21:15*
>
> **£680 protected. One thing needs you:**
> *Rebook via Manchester (21:05, arrives 23:40) — £0*
> *or hold for tomorrow morning — £0*

Calm, complete, and it has already done the work. **That's the moment a client decides they can't travel without you.**

### 4. The Concierge — *always reachable, does real work*

Not a chatbot. It books, changes, cancels, asks the hotel. It already knows your seat, your hotel, your dietary line. It should propose, not wait.

## What dies

| Delete | Why |
|---|---|
| **Wingman Points**, tiers, badges | Gamification is the opposite of luxury |
| **Signals tab** | A feed of what's already handled. If it needs you, it's in the Brief |
| **Insights tab** | ROI is a *moment* after a rescue, not a dashboard you visit |
| **"Share your ROI"** | A concierge does not ask you to advertise them |
| **Stats bar** (miles, nights) | A fitness tracker |
| **Suggestion chips** | Training wheels on a product that claims to already know |
| **Floating pill** | The concierge is already on the Brief |

## What gets promoted

| Promote | Why |
|---|---|
| **The cascade** | It's the product. Give it a full-bleed room. |
| **Standing orders** | *"What I may do without asking you"* — the whole relationship, in one screen |
| **The delegation dial** | Currently buried under "AUTOMATION & DATA". It's the soul: *how much rope do I get?* Ask it during onboarding. |
| **The itinerary** | Should feel like a printed dossier, not a list of rows |
| **Entry requirements** | Real, sourced, never asserted — the thing that saves someone at a border |

## Referral, done properly

Not a coupon. Not points. **An invitation.**

A private office grows by one member introducing another. The copy should read like a letter, not a promo — and the reward should be that *your friend is looked after*, not that you get a discount.

---

## Order of work

1. **Delete the gamification.** Points, tiers, badges, ROI sharing. One afternoon, and it changes what the app *is*.
2. **Build the Situation screen.** The cascade takeover. This is the product.
3. **Rebuild the trip as a dossier** — one document, four chapters, chosen by the calendar.
4. **Collapse to the Brief.** Home becomes today, and only today.
5. **Promote the delegation dial** into onboarding.

Number 1 is free and immediate. Number 2 is the one that matters.
